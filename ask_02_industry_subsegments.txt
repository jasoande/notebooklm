# TIMEOUT: 360
# RETRIES: 3
# VALIDATION: min_words=400, max_words=700
# QUALITY_THRESHOLD: 7.0

You are an industry taxonomy specialist. Based on your research of $name (refer to prior note: "Foundation Research"), identify the precise industry subsegments relevant to this account.

CONTEXT:
- $name operates in: $industry
- Use the company's own business unit structure as a starting point
- Cross-reference with authoritative frameworks:
  * IBISWorld NAICS codes
  * Gartner industry taxonomies
  * McKinsey sector reports
  * Industry-specific associations or regulatory bodies

DELIVERABLE:
Create a table with these columns:

| Subsegment | Description | Relevance to $name | Digital Maturity (1-5) | Revenue Exposure |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|------------------------|------------------|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

INSTRUCTIONS:

1. **Subsegment Identification (5-8 subsegments total)**
   - Focus on subsegments where $name has meaningful operations or revenue
   - Include both traditional and emerging/digital-first categories
   - Avoid overly granular breakdowns (stick to major commercial divisions)

2. **Description (1-2 sentences per subsegment)**
   - Explain the subsegment's scope and business model
   - Clarify its role in the broader industry value chain

3. **Relevance to $name (High/Medium/Low)**
   - HIGH: Core business, >20% revenue contribution
   - MEDIUM: Secondary business, 5-20% revenue
   - LOW: Tangential or emerging, <5% revenue
   - Cite evidence from financial disclosures or business segment breakdowns

4. **Digital Maturity (1-5 scale)**
   - 1 = Traditional, minimal technology adoption
   - 2 = Early digitization (basic automation, legacy systems)
   - 3 = Moderate adoption (cloud migration underway, some modern platforms)
   - 4 = Advanced (containerization, DevOps, data-driven)
   - 5 = Digital-native (AI/ML, real-time systems, API-first architecture)

5. **Revenue Exposure (percentage or descriptor)**
   - Use $name's disclosed segment revenue if available
   - Otherwise estimate: Primary (>50%), Significant (20-50%), Secondary (5-20%), Minimal (<5%)

SPECIAL CATEGORIES:

**Emerging Subsegments** (separate section)
List 2-3 emerging areas that are:
- Currently small but growing rapidly in the industry
- Relevant to $name's future strategy or competitive positioning
- Example: "Direct-to-consumer digital channels" in traditionally B2B industries

**Regulatory & Governance Bodies** (separate section)
- List industry regulators (FDA, SEC, FCC, etc.) if relevant to $name's operations
- Note any self-regulatory associations or voluntary compliance frameworks
- Explain their impact on technology decisions (compliance requirements, data governance)

PRIORITIZATION:
Focus on subsegments with the highest combined score of:
1. Revenue relevance to $name
2. Digital transformation opportunity (higher maturity = more IT spending)
3. Red Hat solution applicability

EXAMPLE ROW:
| Cloud-native Enterprise SaaS | B2B software platforms delivered via cloud with subscription pricing | High - 40% of revenue | 4 - Advanced containerization and microservices | Primary (45%) |

OUTPUT:
- Table format as specified above
- Total word count: 400-600 words including descriptions
- Cite sources for revenue breakdowns: [Source: "$name Annual Report FY2024"]

QUALITY CHECK:
- Are all subsegments distinct (no overlap)?
- Have you avoided generic categories like "Technology" or "Services"?
- Does the total revenue exposure add up to approximately 100%?
